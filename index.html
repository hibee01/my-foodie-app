OVERVIEW

Project Brief

A Platform for users to order food to their doorstep without any hassle.

The Problem

The restaurant franchise had no online presence, as a result customer could not order food to their doorstep as there was no delivery options leaving the restaurant at the mercy of third party apps which often charge high fees - up to 30% in some cases.

The Goal

Developing a food mobile app that allow users order food at their doorstep.

Discover

Design Thinking

The development of Foodie was shaped by the principles of design thinking. It helped me to keep focus on users and their needs at each phase of the design process.

What can users do in the app?

  • The users can order food at their doorstep for themselves or any other user on the app irrespective of their location.
  • They can choose from predefined daily menu of breakfast, lunch and dinner.
  • They can specify their dietary preferences and customize their recipes.
  • They can share details about a meal to a community of users on the app (Locally or Globally).

Personas

Based on interviews, user and online research, I setup two personas.

  • Each persona had a scenario that identified a realistic goal the user might have when working with this app.
  • The information about each persona focused on its goals and frustrations with the app as well as their interactions with it, which drastically affected my design decisions.

Userflow

Before going into user interface design, I created a userflow diagram to map every step of the user interaction required to achieve the main goal of this app. "As a user, I want to purchase a meal through Foodie app"

Visual Design

Designed for Simplification

My aim was a clean, modern look that helps users fufil their goals quickly. "foodie" is a problem-solver; therefore, I focused on simplicity to ensure a smooth flow experience.

User Testing

I used Adobe XD to create clickable high fidelity prototype and tested it with seven Individuals. I gained the following insights. Below are some of the feedbacks from users which was fixed afterwards.

  • Users were curious about the cost of items under the combo/similar sections of a meal page and therefore were reluctant to click the add button as they didnt have enough luxury time to go to each item page just to check their prices.
  • User prefer using other means of payment like wallet, crypto or giftcards rather than inputing their bank details. This lead me to add additional payment option of foodie wallet, crypto.
  • In the sort section of a search result, users wanted specificity in the rating section e.g "1/2/3/4/5 stars" as opposed to "high' or "low" ratings.
  • Users wanted access to the search icon on virtually every page in the app.

what have I learned from this project?

Simplicity is key. As a designer rather than being lured by attractive, trendy and out of the box designs on this project, I remember the "why". The primary goal is to understand the user, their problems and then come up with a design that solves it. Designing this project from start to finish was a positive learning experience.
